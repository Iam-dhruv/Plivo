Our system utilizes a hybrid redundancy sender paired with a zero-jitter, immediate-delivery receiver to minimize latency. The sender implements Forward Error Correction (FEC) with a group size of N=4, appending a single XOR parity packet to the end of each block. To protect the most latency-sensitive frames, the sender explicitly duplicates the first two frames of every block. This hybrid approach keeps bandwidth overhead safely under the strict limit at approximately 1.75x while maximizing proactive recovery. The receiver abandons exact-time thread scheduling, instead instantly forwarding arriving and mathematically reconstructed frames directly to the harness player. By delivering frames immediately, we bypass local operating system scheduler jitter and self-inflicted latency, relying entirely on the harness player's internal deadline buffer. Please grade this implementation at a delay_ms of 120. The system will break if the network experiences severe burst losses that simultaneously drop the base packets, their duplicates, and the block's parity packet. Additionally, extreme out-of-order network delivery may break the playout if the harness strictly expects sequential arrival, as our receiver forwards packets the exact moment they become available.
